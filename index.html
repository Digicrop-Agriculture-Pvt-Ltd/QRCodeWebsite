Forward Pruning :

These shoots after maturity are called as canes, which are pruned after 5th bud position in table grapes and 3-4 bud in wine grapes during September-October for fruits. Hence this pruning is called as forward pruning or fruit pruning.... The same canes are pruned back to 3-4 buds after harvesting the crop in summer.

Bud Breaking :

After pruning, the vine goes dormant. Bud break is when the buds begin to swell and burst open, initiating a new season of vegetative growth. This occurs when temperatures rise and can be influenced by rainfall and irrigation. Applying hydrogen cyanamide (a chemical dormancy breaker) ensures uniform bud break.

Flowering :

These flowers are grouped in bunches called inflorescences. In many species, such as Vitis vinifera, each successfully pollinated flower becomes a grape berry with the inflorescence turning into a cluster of grapes. While the flowers of the grapevines are usually very small, the berries are often large and brightly colored with sweet flavors that attract birds and other animals to disperse the seeds contained within the berries.

Fruit Set:

Fruit set is the stage roughly defined by peppercorn-sized berries (i.e. immediately after bloom), although berries throughout a vineyard, and even within a cluster, are often at different transitional stages

Veraison :

Veraison is not a physiological growth stage, but an artificial designation between the two growth periods of grape berry development.

Ready to Harvest:

All varieties of grape are preclimateric -- they don't ripen once they're taken off the vine. Grapes picked early in the day. Brix of > 18% gives better test.
